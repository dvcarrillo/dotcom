SG Markets WebClear

My journey rethinking Societe Generale’s key platform for real-time cash management, payment tracking, and transaction monitoring

Client Societe Generale CIB
Role Lead designer
Timeline 2023 Present
Focus areas
service design prototyping responsiveness

Challenge

WebClear is a long-standing service within Societe Generale’s Global Transaction Banking suite. In 2023, it began a strategic migration to SG Markets, the bank’s modern platform for corporate and institutional clients. The objective was to modernize the experience, align it with SG Markets’ standards, and support future scalability.

My mandate:

  • Lead the UX vision and workflow redesign.
  • Establish a design-led approach within a team new to structured UX processes.
  • Support technical teams in creating a responsive experience suitable for the platform’s diverse usage contexts.

Approach and design process

1. Framing & discovery

Working closely with product stakeholders, I formalized the problem space, user profiles, and service expectations. We reviewed the existing experience to understand current user paths and documented the functional elements required for the new environment. This allowed me to redesign the experience around clear, streamlined user journeys suitable for SG Markets.

2. Wireframing & navigation model

Based on the newly defined flows, I created wireframes covering both individual screens and the platform-level navigation structure. These were iterated weekly with product owners, developers, and expert users. A key part of this phase was designing responsive behavior, as the new platform needed to support a broader range of screen sizes than before.

3. Prototyping & testing

Once the structure was validated, I built interactive prototypes in Figma aligned with SG Markets’ design system.
To ensure continuity and scalability:

  • I created reusable templates for recurring information patterns.
  • I defined breakpoint behavior early, in collaboration with engineering.
  • We ran usability tests with representative users, refining the experience before development.

4. Iterative delivery

The project progressed in cycles. Each cycle focused on a portion of the service, moving from discovery to validation.
The consistency established early through templates and shared components allowed the team to reduce the effort required for later phases by 50%.

Collaboration

With the product team

Product stakeholders were engaging with a design-led workflow for the first time, so part of my role was to structure collaboration practices:

  • Presenting the UX methodology and expected deliverables.
  • Mapping responsibilities across the design cycle.
  • Visualizing the full plan using shared roadmaps.

Over time, we refined our collaboration rhythm to ensure smooth alignment throughout weekly working sessions.

With the development team

Developers were involved from the earliest design stages. This helped highlight feasibility considerations, edge cases, and responsive constraints early on.
I enriched handoff materials with detailed interaction states and logic to support accurate implementation.
Throughout development, we conducted regular design reviews in testing environments to ensure fidelity between design and final product.

Outcomes and impact

The redesigned experience brought several benefits:

  • A simplified navigation structure, making key actions more direct and intuitive.
  • More consistent layouts that improve scanability and support different types of tasks.
  • A responsive interface validated across multiple screen sizes used by clients.
  • A set of UX metrics through web analytics to evaluate user response to the new design.
  • Clearer feedback and guidance throughout user actions, reinforcing confidence and clarity.
  • Strong alignment with SG Markets’ design system, ensuring cohesion across the broader ecosystem.

This project established a scalable UX foundation for WebClear within SG Markets and strengthened collaborative practices between product, design, and engineering teams.
